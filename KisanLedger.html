Turning Imagery into Insights
Satellite imagery captures light reflected from farmers’ fields, which provides a baseline for crop health and helps indicate anomalies in the growing cycle. Kisan Ledger provides access to real-time satellite imagery, making informed decisions on crop management easier. The satellite imagery captures specific spectral signatures at different stages of the season, which serve as a baseline for crop health and can indicate anomalies in the growing cycle.
Planning
At the planning stage, access to crop productivity maps derived from satellite imagery collections over a multi-year period can be helpful. These maps, when mined for predictive insights, can help farmers optimize field productivity by identifying in-field patterns and localized trends. With this information, agronomists can set site-specific crop production goals and recommend addressing different parts of the field in appropriate ways.

The calendar takes into account weather patterns and other factors, so farmers can make informed decisions about when to plant and harvest their crops.
